### Issue Management Overview

#### What Is Issue Management?

Issue Management in AssistCX provides a structured way to identify, track, and resolve problems that occur during email processing or task execution.

While agents automate most workflows, operational environments can still produce unexpected situations such as incorrect outputs, missing data, or execution failures. Issue Management ensures that these cases are captured, investigated, and resolved systematically.

Instead of handling problems informally through messages or external communication, issues are recorded directly within the platform and linked to the affected email or task. This keeps troubleshooting organized and traceable.

---

#### Purpose of Issue Management

Issue Management helps operational teams maintain reliability across automated workflows.

It allows teams to:

- Report incorrect or failed task outcomes  
- Track and monitor execution errors  
- Investigate root causes of operational problems  
- Collaborate on resolution through comments and updates  
- Maintain an audit trail of issues and actions taken  

By centralizing issue tracking within AssistCX, teams can manage operational exceptions without losing context or visibility.

---

#### Why Issues Occur

Even with automation, certain situations require human intervention.

Issues may occur when input data is incomplete, email content is ambiguous, or external systems behave unexpectedly. They may also arise when business rules change, knowledge sources become outdated, or agent configurations require adjustment.

Capturing these cases as issues ensures they are properly reviewed and addressed rather than overlooked. Over time, this process helps improve workflow reliability and overall system performance.

---

#### Email-Level and Task-Level Issues

Issues can be raised at different levels depending on where the problem occurs in the workflow.

**Email-Level Issues**

Email-level issues are raised when the problem originates from the incoming email itself.

These cases typically involve situations such as unclear or incomplete email content, missing attachments, incorrect classification, or emails that should not have generated a task.

Email-level issues are useful when the source input needs review or correction.

---

**Task-Level Issues**

Task-level issues are raised when the problem occurs during task processing or execution.

Examples include incorrect outputs generated by the agent, failed tool executions, incomplete processing, or errors during data extraction.

Task-level issues focus on problems related to how the system processed the request.

---

#### Why This Distinction Matters

Separating issues by email level and task level helps teams identify the root cause of problems more quickly.

This distinction allows teams to determine whether the issue originated from the incoming request or from the processing logic. As a result, ownership can be assigned more accurately and corrective actions can be taken more effectively.

Over time, this structured approach improves automation reliability and reduces repeated operational failures.
