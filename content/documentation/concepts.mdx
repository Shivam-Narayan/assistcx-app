

## Key Concepts


### Agents

**Think of an Agent as a trained team member.**

An Agent is a configured execution entity responsible for interpreting a Task and determining how it 
should proceed. Each Agent operates within a defined scope and applies structured logic to evaluate 
incoming data. 

Agents do not directly perform system-level actions. Instead, they decide what needs to happen based on 
context, rules, and available capabilities. This separation ensures that decision-making remains 
controlled, auditable, and aligned with business intent.

Agents represent the decision layer of the platform.

---

### Tools

**Think of Tools as the abilities or skills an agent can use.**

A Tool is an executable capability that performs actions on behalf of an Agent. While an Agent determines 
intent, a Tool carries out the operation itself.

A Tool may interact with external systems, update records, retrieve information, validate data, or 
trigger workflows. Tools do not determine strategy or direction; they execute defined actions and return 
results to the Agent for further evaluation.

Tools represent the action layer of the platform and are designed for reuse across multiple workflows to 
ensure consistency and scalability.

---

### Tasks

**Think of a Task as a unit of work created from an email.**

A Task is a structured unit of work created from inbound communication.

Instead of leaving operational requests embedded within emails, AssistCX converts them into traceable 
Tasks with defined lifecycle states. Each Task maintains visibility into status, ownership, execution 
history, and outcomes.

Tasks provide measurability and accountability, forming the operational backbone of the system.

---

### Knowledge

**Think of Knowledge as the reference material agents can read from.**

Knowledge represents the contextual information available to Agents during execution.

This may include policies, documents, structured references, historical records, or domain-specific 
material. When configured, Agents consult Knowledge to ensure outputs are informed, compliant, and 
aligned with organizational standards.

Knowledge provides the contextual intelligence that grounds automation in real business understanding.

---

### Issue Management
**Think of Issue Management as a way to flag and track problems.**

Issue Management governs exception handling within workflows.

When a Task encounters incomplete data, validation failure, or unexpected behavior, an Issue can be 
raised and tracked formally. This ensures that deviations remain visible and actionable rather than 
hidden within automation.

Issue Management strengthens reliability by introducing structured oversight into automated execution.

---

### Integrations

**Think of Integrations as the bridges connecting AssistCX to other systems.**

Integrations connect AssistCX to external systems and services within your technology ecosystem.

Through secure connectors and APIs, the platform interacts with email providers, storage systems, 
enterprise applications, and third-party platforms. Rather than replacing existing infrastructure, 
AssistCX orchestrates execution across it.

Integrations extend automation beyond the platform while maintaining governance and control.
