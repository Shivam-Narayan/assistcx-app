#### Documents

Documents are the individual files stored within a knowledge collection.  
They contain the actual reference material that agents use when retrieving information during task execution.

While collections organize knowledge at a high level, documents provide the detailed content that agents consult when answering questions, validating information, or generating outputs.

When a task requires additional context, the agent searches the linked collections and retrieves relevant information from the documents stored within them.

This ensures that responses are grounded in organizational knowledge rather than relying only on task input or email content.

---

##### Role of Documents in Agent Execution

Documents act as the primary source of factual information for agents.

During task processing, agents may retrieve relevant sections of documents to support decision-making or generate accurate outputs. This allows agents to reference internal documentation, policies, or operational guidelines when handling requests.

For example, documents may contain:

- Company policies and procedures  
- Product documentation and specifications  
- Support knowledge bases or FAQs  
- Compliance guidelines or regulatory rules  
- Internal process documentation  

By referencing these documents, agents can produce responses that align with the organization's standards and knowledge.

---

##### Adding Documents to a Collection

Documents are uploaded and stored within collections so that they can be accessed during task execution.

When adding documents, users typically:

1. Select the target collection where the document should be stored.  
2. Upload the document file.  
3. Provide metadata such as title, description, or tags to help classify the document.

Once added, the document becomes searchable within that collection and can be referenced by agents linked to the collection.

---

##### Supported Formats

AssistCX supports common document formats that contain readable text content.

Supported formats typically include:

- PDF  
- DOCX  
- TXT  
- CSV  
- Other text-based document formats  

Documents that contain structured or clearly written text generally perform best for retrieval.

Files that rely heavily on images or complex layouts may require additional processing for optimal results.

---

##### Document Management

Documents within a collection can be maintained as knowledge evolves.

Users can update documents when policies change, replace outdated files with new versions, or remove documents that are no longer relevant. Maintaining up-to-date documents ensures that agents reference accurate information during task execution.

Proper document organization helps keep knowledge collections reliable and easy to manage.
