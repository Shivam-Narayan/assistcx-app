#### Collections

Collections organize knowledge into logical groups. Each collection contains documents or reference material related to a specific topic, workflow, or operational domain.

You can think of a collection as a structured container that keeps related knowledge together so it can be managed and retrieved easily by agents.

Examples of collections include:

- Support FAQs  
- Finance policies  
- Vendor guidelines  
- HR documentation  

Organizing knowledge into collections helps ensure that agents access relevant information during task execution.

---

##### Managing Collections

Collections can be created, updated, or removed as knowledge evolves.

Teams may create separate collections for different departments, workflows, or operational domains. Existing collections can be updated when documents change or when the organization of knowledge needs to be refined.

Collections that are no longer relevant can be deleted to keep the knowledge base clean and manageable.

---

##### Metadata and Knowledge Topics

Metadata provides additional structure that helps classify and retrieve knowledge more effectively.

Metadata may include:

- Tags or labels that describe the content
- Topics or categories that group related information
- Descriptions that explain the purpose of the collection
- Ownership information identifying the responsible team

Well-defined metadata improves search accuracy and helps agents retrieve the most relevant information during execution.
